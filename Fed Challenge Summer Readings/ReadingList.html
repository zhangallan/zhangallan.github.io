1 Week of <2015-06-27 Sat>

1.1 Why is it taking longer and longer to fill open jobs?

  • Very good high level primer on vacancy duration and wage inflation. Definitely encouraged to check out the links in the article

1.2 Falling Oil Prices and Global Saving

  • I don't particularly find this too interesting, but the topic has ramifications on the inflation rate and could come up

1.3 Last Greek thoughts

  • Really good post on Greece and its relationships with the ECB by Cochrane. Don't know how much of this will still be relevant come fall, but the overall monetary themes are really interesting

1.4 Dovish Fed

  • Tim Duy. Amazing as always

1.5 June FOMC Recap

  • Tim Duy's report on the June FOMC Statement.

1.6 U.S. Economy in a Snapshot

  • FRBNY is putting this monthly presentation out now, along with the accompanying data. Very little actual economic analysis, but a good summary.

1.7 The Passthrough of Labor Costs to Price Inflation

  • Still have yet to read it, but it looks like an interesting paper. Most interesting point is that there isn't much of a passthrough from labor to inflation. This could imply that wage inflation isn't a particularly strong predictor for overall inflation. Also of note is that the paper was authored by economists at the Board of Governors in DC.

1.8 Will the Elevated Share of Part-Time Workers Last?

  • From cyclical to structural unemployment. If the Fed can't do much more on the labor side of its dual mandate, then most of the focus will be on the inflation side… Where the Fed is currently vastly undershooting, but long term inflation expectations have held steady. What to do…

1.9 Interpreting the yield curve: Pessimism or precaution?

  • Okay technically about the UK, but still a good discussion on the factors that affect the curve.

1.10 The Long Unwind of Excess Money Demand

  • Somewhat speculative in nature with little research to back it up, and I'm not totally convinced about the argument, but it's food for thought. Also Kevin Erdmann and Beckworth have a good back and forth in the comments
